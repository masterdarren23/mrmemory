### Notes on Category 4+ US Landfalls
Historical Cat 4 US landfalls (CONUS): Andrew (1992), Charley (2004), Harvey (2017), Laura (2020), Ian (2022), Milton (2024) — plus several pre-1950. Approximately **1 per ~10 years** in modern era, but clustering occurs.

### Cat 5 US Landfalls (ever recorded)
Only **4 total**: Labor Day 1935, Camille (1969), Andrew (1992 — reclassified), Michael (2018). Rate: ~1 per 22 years, or **~4.5% chance per year**.

## Pre-Season Storm Formation (before June 1)

### Named Storms Before Official Season Start (1950-2025)
- Pre-season named storms occurred in: ~30-35% of years in recent decades (increasing trend)
- **April named storms:** ~5-8% of years historically; ~10-12% in 2010-2025 era
- **Pre-season hurricanes (before June 1):** Extremely rare — ~2-3% of years
- **Pre-season US hurricane landfall (before June 1):** Essentially 0% — no modern records

### Relevant to Active Polymarket Markets:
- "Named storm forms before hurricane season" (Apr 7 - May 31): **Historical base rate ~30-40%** (the 2025 market resolved NO)
- "Hurricane forms by May 31": **Historical base rate ~3-5%** (very rare pre-season hurricanes)
- "Hurricane makes landfall in US by May 31": **~0-1%** (essentially never happens)

## Annual Temperature Records

### Global Hottest Year Records (ERA5, 1940-2025)
- 2023, 2024, 2025 were consecutive record-breaking years
- P(any given year beats prior record) historically: ~10-15%, but recent trend suggests ~30-40% under current warming trajectory
- P(2026 is hottest year ever): Dependent on ENSO phase — La Niña/neutral years are less likely to set records. Estimate: **15-25%**

### US Temperature Records
- Monthly records broken per year (any station): Essentially 100% — some station breaks a record every month
- All-time US national heat record (Death Valley 134°F): Extremely unlikely to be broken
- Probability of "hottest summer on record" for US in any given year: ~8-12% (higher recently)

## Natural Disaster Probabilities (for Polymarket "Natural Disaster in 2026" market)

The market requires ONE of:
1. **Cat 5 US landfall:** ~3-4.5% per year
2. **Major meteor strike (10kt+):** ~0.5-1% per year (based on Chelyabinsk-class events every ~50-100 years)
3. **VEI ≥6 eruption:** ~0.1% per year (last was Pinatubo 1991; average interval ~50-100 years)
4. **M8.5+ earthquake:** ~0.5-1% per year globally

**Combined P(at least one):** ~5-7% per year (summing independent rare events)

The market is at 47% YES — this appears **significantly overpriced** vs. the ~5-7% historical base rate.
