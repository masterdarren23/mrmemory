# NOAA CPC Seasonal Outlooks — February 2026

*Fetched: 2026-02-15 from cpc.ncep.noaa.gov*

## ENSO Status (as of Feb 12, 2026)

- **Current:** La Niña Advisory active; Niño-3.4 at -0.9°C
- **Transition:** 60% chance La Niña → ENSO-neutral in Feb-Apr 2026
- **Summer:** ENSO-neutral likely persisting (56% in JJA 2026)
- **Late 2026:** 50-60% chance El Niño forming late summer/fall — but model uncertainty is high and spring barrier limits accuracy
- Subsurface temperatures sharply increasing — warm Kelvin wave propagating east

## Current Seasonal Outlooks (Issued Jan 15, 2026)

### Temperature — FMA 2026 (Feb-Mar-Apr)
- **Below normal:** Northern Rockies, Northern Plains, Upper Mississippi Valley, interior PNW
- **Above normal:** California, Southwest, Southern Plains, Lower Mississippi Valley, Southeast, parts of Ohio Valley/Mid-Atlantic
- **Highest probabilities (50-60%):** Southwest and Southeast CONUS
- **Alaska:** Above-normal West/Northwest/North; below-normal SE mainland and SE Alaska

### Precipitation — FMA 2026
- **Above normal:** Northern Rockies, Northern Plains, Upper Mississippi Valley, Upper Midwest, Great Lakes, Ohio Valley, interior Northeast
- **Below normal:** California, Southwest, Southern Rockies, Southern Plains, Lower Mississippi Valley, Southeast, southern Mid-Atlantic
- **Alaska:** Above-normal western third; below-normal southern coast and SE Alaska

### February 2026 Monthly Outlook (Issued Jan 31, 2026)
- **Key driver:** Prolonged negative Arctic Oscillation (AO) → widespread cold outbreaks east of Rockies
- **Below-normal temps:** Almost entire eastern third of CONUS; strongest signal (>50%) Mid-Atlantic, Appalachians, Northeast, Ohio Valley
- **Above-normal temps:** Western CONUS (especially Great Basin/Southwest), Florida Peninsula (>50%), Southern Plains
- **Precip below normal:** California, Great Basin, Central Rockies, Central Plains, parts of Southeast
- **Precip above normal:** Northern Rockies/Plains, Great Lakes, Ohio Valley

## Seasonal Drought Outlook (Updated Feb 2026)

- **Drought persists/develops:** Southwest, Central-Southern Great Plains, Florida Peninsula
- **Drought removal/improvement:** Pacific NW, Northern Intermountain West, Northern Rockies (but snowpack concerns)
- **Improvement:** Eastern Arkansas, northern Mississippi through Midwest
- **Persistence:** Mid-Atlantic/Northeast hydrological drought — slow recovery expected
- **Hawaii:** Wet pattern, continued improvement
- **Alaska:** Expected drought-free through April

## Longer-Range Signals (Mar 2026 → Feb 2027)

- **MAM 2026:** La Niña impacts wane; above-normal temps expand over West, Southern Plains, East Coast
- **JJA 2026:** Widespread above-normal temps across most of CONUS
- **Late 2026:** Above-normal temps retreat to southern tier and East Coast
- **Precip:** Below-normal precip shifts from SW → NW in summer; above-normal shifts from Great Lakes → SE/East Coast

## Key Patterns for Trading

1. **ENSO transition timing** is the dominant signal — La Niña ending, potential El Niño by late 2026
2. **Negative AO in Feb 2026** = cold outbreaks in eastern US (relevant for any temperature record markets)
3. **Drought expansion** in Southern Plains and Florida — relevant for agricultural/disaster markets
4. **Summer 2026 heat signal** is strong across CONUS — if "hottest year" markets emerge, above-normal is heavily favored
5. **Hurricane season implications:** Transition from La Niña to neutral/El Niño reduces Atlantic hurricane activity — fewer storms expected vs. 2024-2025 hyperactive seasons
