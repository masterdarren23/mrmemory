# Becker Research: The Microstructure of Wealth Transfer in Prediction Markets
## Key Findings for Our Trading Strategy

Source: https://jbecker.dev/research/prediction-market-microstructure
Dataset: 72.1M trades, $18.26B volume (Kalshi data, applicable to Polymarket)

---

## 1. THE MAKER-TAKER WEALTH TRANSFER
- **Makers earn +1.12% excess return per trade; Takers lose -1.12%**
- This is THE fundamental dynamic: wealth flows from takers to makers
- **We must be MAKERS, not TAKERS** — place limit orders, don't market buy

## 2. WEATHER CATEGORY SPECIFICS
- Weather shows a **2.57 pp maker-taker gap** (4.4M trades analyzed)
- Taker return: **-1.29%** | Maker return: **+1.29%**
- Weather is MORE inefficient than Politics (1.02 pp) and Sports (2.23 pp)
- Weather is LESS efficient than Finance (0.17 pp)
- **Weather attracts emotional/uninformed bettors** → our edge is real

## 3. THE LONGSHOT BIAS
- Contracts priced at 5¢ win only 4.18% of the time (should win 5%)
- Contracts at 95¢ win 95.83% (outperform)
- All contracts below 20¢ underperform their implied odds
- All contracts above 80¢ outperform their implied odds
- **Implication: Buy high-probability NO shares (80¢+) = structural edge**

## 4. THE YES/NO ASYMMETRY ("OPTIMISM TAX")
- YES contracts underperform NO at **69 of 99 price levels**
- At 1¢: YES EV = -41%, NO EV = +23% (64pp gap!)
- Dollar-weighted: YES buyers return -1.02%, NO buyers +0.83%
- **Takers disproportionately buy YES** at longshot prices
- **Implication: Sell into YES demand. Be the NO side.**

## 5. MAKERS DON'T NEED TO PREDICT THE FUTURE
- Maker returns are nearly identical regardless of YES/NO direction
- Makers profit through STRUCTURE, not FORECASTING
- They just need to be the counterparty to optimism
- **"Makers do not profit by knowing which way to bet, but through some mechanism that applies equally to both directions"**

## 6. PROFESSIONALIZATION MATTERS
- Pre-2024: takers won (+2.0%), makers lost (-2.0%)
- Post-2024 election: makers win (+2.5%), takers lose
- **Volume attracts sophisticated makers → makes extraction possible**
- Weather has less sophisticated competition than Sports/Politics

## STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS FOR OUR $100

### Primary Strategy: BE A MAKER
- Place limit orders (don't market buy)
- Collect spread + liquidity rewards
- Provide liquidity at prices informed by our weather models

### Secondary Strategy: EXPLOIT YES BIAS
- When takers buy YES on weather catastrophes (hurricanes, records), sell into them
- Buy NO shares via limit orders at favorable prices
- Weather takers are emotional (fear-driven) → 2.57 pp gap to exploit

### Tertiary Strategy: BUY HIGH-PROB NO SHARES
- Longshot bias means contracts >80¢ outperform
- Weather catastrophe NO shares at 85-95¢ = structural edge
- Compounded small gains over many positions

### What NOT To Do
- Don't market-buy (you become a taker, lose 1.29% structurally)
- Don't buy YES longshots (worst EV in the market)
- Don't trade emotionally or chase narratives
