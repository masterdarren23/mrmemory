## Historical Pattern Analysis

### Resolved Weather Markets (observed patterns)
- "Named storm forms before hurricane season" (2025): Resolved NO at ~32% starting price
- Markets with binary "will X happen" structure consistently overprice dramatic outcomes
- Low-liquidity markets are slower to correct mispricings

### Price Movement Patterns
- **Pre-season (Jan-May):** Weather markets are relatively stable; prices reflect "base rate + recency bias"
- **Early season (Jun-Jul):** Prices begin moving as first tropical disturbances form; often overreact to every invest area
- **Peak season (Aug-Oct):** Maximum volatility; YES prices spike on every named storm, especially if approaching US
- **Post-season (Nov-Dec):** Markets converge toward resolution; NO prices rise as time runs out

### Volume Patterns
- Weather markets see volume spikes:
  - When NOAA releases seasonal hurricane outlook (~May)
  - When named storms form
  - When storms threaten US landfall
  - Just before resolution deadlines

### Mispricing Triggers
1. **Availability bias after major storms:** After 2024-2025 active seasons, markets dramatically overweight repeat
2. **Anchoring on recent seasons:** Traders anchor on 2024's hyperactivity rather than long-term base rates
3. **Conjunction fallacy:** "Natural Disaster" market combines 4 independent rare events but prices as if any one is ~47% likely
4. **Neglect of ENSO transition:** La Niña → neutral/El Niño should reduce hurricane activity, but market hasn't adjusted
